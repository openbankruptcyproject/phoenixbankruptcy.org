Arizona Exemptions for Glendale Residents

Glendale residents file in the District of Arizona, Phoenix Division. Arizona's $250,000 homestead exemption ($500,000 married) protects most Glendale homes, which tend to be more affordable than East Valley properties. The $6,000 vehicle exemption covers most vehicle equity.

Exemptions determine what property you can keep in bankruptcy. See the full Arizona exemptions guide.

What to Expect Filing from Glendale

The Phoenix courthouse at 230 N 1st Ave is approximately 10 miles southeast -- one of the shortest commutes among Phoenix suburbs. After filing, the automatic stay immediately stops wage garnishments, collection calls, lawsuits, and foreclosure.

The Filing Process

  1. Credit counseling -- Complete an approved course within 180 days before filing ($15-25). Available online 24/7.
  2. File your petition -- Your petition is filed with the District of Arizona, Phoenix Division. The automatic stay takes effect immediately, stopping collections, garnishments, and foreclosures.
  3. 341 meeting -- About 30 days after filing, attend a brief meeting of creditors. Bring photo ID and your Social Security card. Many meetings are now by phone.
  4. Debtor education -- Complete a second financial course before discharge ($10-25).
  5. Discharge -- Chapter 7: approximately 60 days after the 341 meeting. Chapter 13: after completing your 3-5 year repayment plan.

Frequently Asked Questions -- Glendale, AZ

How close is the bankruptcy courthouse to Glendale?

The courthouse at 230 N 1st Ave, Phoenix, AZ 85003 is approximately 10 miles away -- one of the shortest commutes among Phoenix suburbs.

Can I stop wage garnishment by filing?

Yes. Filing triggers the automatic stay, immediately stopping all garnishments. In Arizona, creditors can garnish up to 25% of disposable wages. Once you file, this stops.

Is my mobile home protected?

Yes. Arizona's homestead exemption applies to mobile homes and manufactured homes that are your primary residence. The $250,000 exemption ($500,000 married) applies regardless of dwelling type.
