District of Arizona

District of Arizona (D. Ariz.)

Arizona has a single federal judicial district covering the entire state. All bankruptcy cases in Arizona are filed in the District of Arizona, regardless of where you live. The court has divisions in Phoenix and Tucson, with additional locations in Yuma and Prescott.

Phoenix Courthouse: 230 N 1st Ave, Phoenix, AZ 85003

Tucson Courthouse: 38 S Scott Ave, Tucson, AZ 85701

Divisional offices: Phoenix, Tucson, Yuma, Prescott

Frequently Asked Questions

Which bankruptcy district covers Phoenix?

Phoenix is in the District of Arizona, which is the only federal bankruptcy district in the state. The courthouse is located at 230 N 1st Ave, Phoenix, AZ 85003. All Arizona zip codes (850xx-865xx) are in this district.

Does Arizona have more than one bankruptcy district?

No. Arizona has a single federal judicial district - the District of Arizona. All bankruptcy cases in the state are filed here, regardless of where you live. The court has divisions in Phoenix, Tucson, Yuma, and Prescott.

Can I file bankruptcy in a different district than where I live?

Generally no. You must file in the district where you have lived for the greater part of the last 180 days, where your principal assets are located, or where your domicile is. Filing in the wrong district can result in your case being dismissed or transferred.
