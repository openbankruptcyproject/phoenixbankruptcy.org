Bankruptcy by County -- Phoenix Metro Area

Your county determines which federal court handles your bankruptcy case and which state exemptions protect your property. Find your county below for specific information.

3 Counties in the Phoenix Metro

Maricopa County

Maricopa County is the most populous county in Arizona, containing Phoenix and most of its suburbs including Scottsdale, Tempe, Mesa, Chandler, and Gilbert. It handles the vast majority of Arizona bankruptcy filings.

District: District of Arizona (Phoenix Division)
Courthouse: 230 N 1st Ave, Phoenix, AZ 85003
Maricopa County bankruptcy guide →

Pinal County

Pinal County lies between Phoenix and Tucson and includes fast-growing cities like Casa Grande, Florence, and parts of the San Tan Valley. Cases are filed in the Phoenix Division of the District of Arizona.

District: District of Arizona (Phoenix Division)
Courthouse: 230 N 1st Ave, Phoenix, AZ 85003
Pinal County bankruptcy guide →

Yavapai County

Yavapai County is in central Arizona, home to Prescott and Sedona. While part of the greater Phoenix metro for some purposes, it has its own division within the District of Arizona.

District: District of Arizona (Prescott Division)
Courthouse: 1 E Butte St, Prescott, AZ 86301 (Note: most hearings may be conducted in Phoenix or by phone)

Frequently Asked Questions

Which counties are in the Phoenix bankruptcy metro area?

The Phoenix metro area covers 3 counties: Maricopa County, Pinal County, Yavapai County. Each county falls under a specific federal bankruptcy court district.

Does it matter which county I live in for bankruptcy?

Yes. Your county determines which federal district court handles your case, and potentially which state exemptions apply. Different districts may have different local rules, trustees, and dismissal rates.
